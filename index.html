Product Case Studies

I tear down products to understand what they got right, what they got wrong, and what I'd change.

PERPLEXITY AI
The Citation Trust Problem
Perplexity is the most interesting AI product of 2024 — but it has a trust problem nobody's solving. It cites sources, yet 60% of users never click them. The real issue: the UI treats citations as footnotes, not as confidence signals. I'd redesign the answer card to show source credibility inline, turning "trust me" into "here's why you can trust this."
AI Trust Search UX Hallucination Risk
NOTION AI
The Co-pilot Positioning Trap
Notion AI is a $10/month add-on sitting inside a tool people already pay for — and it shows. Usage drops after week 2 because AI is a feature, not a workflow. The fix isn't better prompts — it's proactive AI that surfaces suggestions without being asked. Think: "You have 3 meeting notes with no action items. Want me to extract them?" That's a habit-forming product, not a chatbox.
AI Product Habit Design Retention
LINKEDIN
AI Reply Feature
LinkedIn's AI reply suggestions are generic enough to be useless and specific enough to feel creepy. The product problem: it optimizes for engagement, not authentic connection. The fix isn't better prompts — it's rethinking when AI should suggest vs. stay silent.
AI Product Social Trust Engagement
YOUTUBE MUSIC
Discovery UX Analysis
YouTube Music has the best catalogue and the worst discovery. The recommendation engine pushes familiar artists 80% of the time — killing serendipity. I'd redesign the home tab with a "Discover" mode that surfaces genre-adjacent artists, not just more of what you already play.
Recommendations Discovery Retention
